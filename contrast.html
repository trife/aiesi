Two ways to make a computer "see"

Hub Materials Demos Teachable Machine Learned vs. Rules
✍️

You wrote the rules by hand

The browser demos. Every demo is based on a threshold or a formula you set with a slider. Nobody trained anything; you are the model.

How it fails: the "right" threshold doesn't exist. Moving the slider changes the output, with no signal telling you which value is correct. Shadows, clumps, and lighting can break hand-written rules just as easily as they break a trained model.

🧠

You trained it by example

Teachable Machine. You showed it fruit examples and it found the pattern on its own. Nobody wrote down what an "apple" or "banana" looks like; the model inferred it from your photos.

How it fails: it learns whatever's in the training photos, which isn't every object possible. Lighting, background, angle can be what is identified as "the pattern" instead of the fruit itself.

Both need validation and neither approach knows when it's wrong. A trained model gives a confidence score that doesn't mean what it sounds like it means. A hand-written rule gives a precise-looking number that's only as good as its threshold. In both cases, the "trust check" is the same: does this match what a person looking carefully would say?
