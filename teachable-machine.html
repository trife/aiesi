The activity

  1. 1

    Train

    Create an Image Project → Standard image model. Make two classes - e.g. "healthy leaf" vs. "diseased leaf," or two different species. Use your webcam or upload a handful of photos per class (10–20 is plenty to start). Click Train Model.

  2. 2

    Test

    Use the live preview panel to show it new leaves from the same conditions you trained on.

  3. 3

    Break it

    Now feed it edge cases on purpose. The confidence score rarely drops to "I don't know." It answers confidently even when it shouldn't.

Discuss

A few prompts to run as a group after everyone has broken their model:

  • What did your model actually learn - the leaf, or something incidental in the background/lighting of your training photos?
  • Was your training set balanced between classes? What happens to a model trained on 18 healthy photos and 4 diseased ones?
  • The confidence score was high even on nonsense inputs. What would you need to add to this workflow to catch that in a real classroom or research use?
